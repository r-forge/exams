  1. Question

    The following figure shows a scatterplot. Which of the following statements are correct?

    1. The absolute value of the correlation coefficient is at least 0.8.
    2. The slope of the regression line is about 1.
    3. The standard deviation of Y is at least 6.
    4. For X=0.3, Y can be expected to be about 0 .
    5. The mean of X is at most 5.

    Solution


    1. False. No association between the variables is observed in the scatterplot. This implies a correlation coefficient close to 0.
    2. False. The slope of the regression line is given by r· sy / sx and hence not about equal to 1.
    3. False. The standard deviation of Y is about equal to 1 and is therefore smaller than 6.
    4. True. The regression line at X=0.3 implies a value of about Y=0.
    5. True. The mean of X is about equal to 0 and hence is smaller than 5.
