Question

Given two points p=(2,3)p = (2, 3) and q=(5,2)q = (5, 2) in a Cartesian coordinate system:


  1. What is the Manhattan distance d1(p,q)d_1(p, q)?
  2. What is the Euclidean distance d2(p,q)d_2(p, q)?
  3. What is the maximum distance d∞(p,q)d_\infty(p, q)?

Solution

The distances are visualized below in green (d1d_1), red (d2d_2), and blue (d∞d_\infty).


  1. d1(p,q)=∑i|pi−qi|=|2−5|+|3−2|=4d_1(p, q) = \sum_i |p_i - q_i| = |2 - 5| + |3 - 2| = 4.
  2. d2(p,q)=∑i(pi−qi)2=(2−5)2+(3−2)2=3.162d_2(p, q) = \sqrt{\sum_i (p_i - q_i)^2} = \sqrt{(2 - 5)^2 + (3 - 2)^2} = 3.162.
  3. d∞(p,q)=maxi|pi−qi|=max(|2−5|,|3−2|)=3d_\infty(p, q) = \max_i |p_i - q_i| = \max(|2 - 5|, |3 - 2|) = 3.
