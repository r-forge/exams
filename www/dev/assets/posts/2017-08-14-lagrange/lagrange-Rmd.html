Exam 1

  1. Question

    A firm has the following production function: F(K,L)=KL3. F(K,L)= K L^{3}. The price for one unit of capital is pK=20p_K = 20 and the price for one unit of labor is pL=11p_L = 11. Minimize the costs of the firm considering its production function and given a target production output of 730 units.

    How high are in this case the minimal costs?


    Solution

    Step 1: Formulating the minimization problem. minK,LC(K,L)=pKK+pLL=20K+11Lsubject to:F(K,L)=QKL3=730 \begin{aligned} \min_{K,L} C(K,L) & = p_K K + p_L L\\ & = 20 K + 11 L\\ \mbox{subject~to:} & F(K,L) = Q \\ & K L^{3} = 730 \end{aligned} Step 2: Lagrange function. ℒ(K,L,λ)=C(K,L)−λ(F(K,L)−Q)=20K+11L−λ(KL3−730) \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}(K, L, \lambda) & = C(K, L) - \lambda (F(K, L) - Q) \\ & = 20 K + 11 L - \lambda (K L^{3} -730) \end{aligned} Step 3: First order conditions. ∂ℒ∂K=20−λL3=0∂ℒ∂L=11−3λKL3−1=0∂ℒ∂λ=−(KL3−730)=0 \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial K} & = 20 - \lambda L^{3} = 0\\ \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial L} & = 11 - {3} \lambda K L^{3 - 1} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial \lambda} & = -(K L^{3}-730) = 0 \end{aligned} Step 4: Solve the system of equations for KK, LL, and λ\lambda.

    Solving the first two equations for λ\lambda and equating them gives: 20L3=113KL3−1K=113⋅20⋅L3−(3−1)K=1160⋅L \begin{aligned} \frac{20}{L^{3}} & = \frac{11}{{3} K L^{3 - 1}}\\ K & = \frac{11}{3 \cdot 20} \cdot L^{3 - (3 - 1)}\\ K & = \frac{11}{60} \cdot L \end{aligned} Substituting this in the optimization constraint gives: KL3=730(1160⋅L)L3=7301160L4=730L=(6011⋅730)14=7.9436547≈7.94K=1160⋅L=1.4563367≈1.46 \begin{aligned} K L^{3} & = 730\\ \left(\frac{11}{60}\cdot L \right) L^{3} & = 730\\ \frac{11}{60} L^{4} & = 730\\ L & = \left(\frac{60}{11} \cdot 730\right)^{\frac{1}{4}} = 7.9436547 \approx 7.94\\ K & = \frac{11}{60} \cdot L = 1.4563367 \approx 1.46 \end{aligned}

    The minimal costs can be obtained by substituting the optimal factor combination in the objective function: C(K,L)=20K+11L=29.126734+87.380201=116.506935≈116.51 \begin{aligned} C(K, L) & = 20 K + 11 L\\ & = 29.126734 + 87.380201 \\ & = 116.506935 \approx 116.51 \end{aligned}

    Given the target output, the minimal costs are 116.51116.51.
